1. Texas Executor Property Sale Checklist

1. Asset Purpose & Scope

This checklist provides a structured process to support the sale of real property by an executor or personal representative in Texas. It is designed to help manage procedural risk, documentation gaps, and coordination issues that commonly arise during estate-related property sales.

This asset does not provide legal advice, interpret probate orders, or replace attorney guidance. It supports operational clarity only.

⸻

2. Appropriate Use Conditions

Use this checklist when:
	•	An executor or administrator is responsible for selling estate property
	•	The property is subject to Texas probate or estate administration
	•	Multiple parties (heirs, attorneys, agents) are involved

This asset alone is insufficient when court interpretation or contested probate matters are present.

⸻

3. Risk Areas Addressed
	•	Authority misalignment
	•	Heir interference
	•	Incomplete documentation
	•	Premature marketing
	•	Miscommunication between parties

⸻

4. Core Checklist
	1.	Confirm executor authority documentation is available
	2.	Verify property ownership status within estate records
	3.	Identify all known heirs and interested parties
	4.	Confirm whether court approval is required prior to sale
	5.	Coordinate with estate attorney regarding sale constraints
	6.	Secure and document property condition
	7.	Establish a single point of communication
	8.	Maintain written records of all decisions
	9.	Confirm net proceeds handling instructions
	10.	Retain closing documentation within estate file

⸻

5. Common Failure Points This Asset Helps Surface
	•	Marketing property before authority is confirmed
	•	Allowing heirs to negotiate independently
	•	Missing required court permissions
	•	Incomplete disclosure documentation
	•	Poor record retention

⸻

6. Documentation & Recordkeeping Guidance

Maintain copies of:
	•	Letters Testamentary or Administration
	•	Probate orders affecting sale
	•	Communications with heirs
	•	Listing and closing documents

Records should be retained within the estate file and accessible to authorized parties only.

⸻

7. Professional Judgment Boundary

This checklist supports process awareness but does not determine legal sufficiency or compliance.

⸻

8. Disclaimer

Standard disclaimer applied.
